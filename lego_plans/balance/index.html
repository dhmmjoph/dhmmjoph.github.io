Finding Factors

By placing different numbers of 2x1 bricks at different distances from the pivot point, we can cause the scale to still be balanced. For example, if we place a single brick 10 studs from the center, and two bricks 5 studs from the center, the balance of the scale will be maintained.

In general, \(n_1\) bricks \(d_1\) studs from the center will be balanced by \(n_2\) bricks \(d_2\) studs from the center if, and only if, \( n_1 \times d_1 = n_2 \times d_2\). In other words, the scale will be balanced if the pairs \( (n_1,d_1) \) and \( (n_2,d_2) \) are sets of factors of the same number.

Not all brick arrangements on one side of the scale can be balanced by different arrangements on the other side. For example, a single brick 3 studs from the center could not be balanced by any other number of bricks at any other distance, since there are no paris of whole numbers other than \( (1,3) \) that multiply to 3. (By definition, prime numbers' only factors are themselves and 1).

Questions

Try to answer these questions either mathematically or by experimenting with the scale:

  1. Fill in the empty cells in the following table with the values necessary to balance the scale:
    \(n_1\) \(d_1\) \(n_2\) \(d_2\)
    1 4 2
    1 4 4
    2 8 16
    2 3 4
    9 3 6
  2. List as many balanced configurations as possible given that (a) the balance beam extends 20 studs from the center, (b) 8 or fewer bricks are used, and (c) the left side of the scale has fewer bricks than the right side

Notes

9689 Brick Mass

If doing this activity with the 9689 set as suggested above, note that, while the set includes 8 total 2x2 bricks, they are not all the same type:

Using eight of either type of brick works fine, but mixing the types is not advised, since the bricks have different masses.

Slight Imprecisions

Note also that the pivot point is not on a stud, but halfway in between two studs. This means, for example, that a position of 8 studs from the center is not twice as far as a position of 4 studs from the center. However, the difference is small enough that balancing the scale is still easily possible.
