--- layout: LEGO title: Mystery Object subhead: Pairs of students use diagram reading and communications skills to reconstruct provided LEGO models. --- Adapted & expanded from the original plan by Linda Hamilton.

Activity 1

Design a simple model using normal or DUPLO bricks and model it in LDD or LDraw. Take screenshots of the model from front, top, and side perspectives. Make sure that every part on the model is visible in enough perspectives to reconstruct its position exactly.

Provide pairs of students with the parts necessary to reconstruct the model and a printout of the perspectives. Have students reconstruct the original model, looking only at the provided perspective views.

Activity 2

Design a simple model and generate perspective views as above. Divide students into pairs. Within each pair, student A may look at the perspective views, but may not touch or point at the parts or the constructed model. Student B may touch, manipulate, and assemble the parts, but may not look at the perspective views. Thus, both students must communicate verbally to reconstruct the model.

Activity 3

(This is similar to the Science Olympiad event "Write it, Do it".)

Divide students into pairs. Show a simple LEGO model to one student from each pair. They must write a set of "instructions" to recreate the model, which are limited to plain text and may not include any diagrams or images. These instructions are then given to their partners, who have not seen the reference model and must recreate it referencing only their partner's written instructions.
