--- layout: LEGO title: Hollow Pyramid Answers --- Below are answers to the hollow pyramid challenge problems given on the Triangle & Pyramid Math page.

How many bricks are in layer \(i\) of a hollow pyramid?

Each layer is a hollow square of length \( i \times i \). So, a reasonable guess for the number of bricks in an \( i \times i \) hollow square is \( 4i \). However, the 4 bricks in the corners of the square are each part of 2 sides, and so are double-counted by the above guess. Thus, the total number of bricks in layer \( i \) is: $$ 4i - 4 $$

Notice that this expression predicts that the first layer of a hollow pyramid has \( (4)(1) - 4 = 0 \) bricks, which is obviously false. However, the expression gives the correct result for all other values of \( n \). Thus, technically the number of bricks in layer i of a hollow pyramid is the piecewise function: $$ b(i) = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} 1 & i = 1\\ 4i - 4 & i > 1\\ \end{array} \right. $$

How can we express the total number of bricks in a pyramid of height \(n\) as a sum?

Since layer 1 has 1 brick, and each subsequent layer \(i\) has \(4i-4\) bricks, the total number of bricks could be calculated as: $$ 1 + \sum_{i=2}^n (4i-4) $$ Since \( 4i-4 = 0 \) for \(i=1\), this is equal to: $$ 1 + \sum_{i=1}^n (4i-4) $$ which will make things easier when calculating the closed-form solution below.

What is the closed-form solution to that sum?

By using the properties of summation notation, we can break the sum into simpler sums: $$ 1 + \sum_{i=1}^n (4i-4) = 1 + 4\sum_{i=1}^n i - \sum_{i=1}^n 4 $$ As we saw earlier, \( \sum_{i=1}^n i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \). Further, \( \sum_{i=1}^n 4 = 4n \). Then our expression becomes: $$ 1 + 4 \left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right) - 4n $$ From there, some relatively simple algebra can be used to simplify the expression: $$ \begin{eqnarray} & & 1 + 4 \left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right) - 4n \\ & = & 2n(n+1) - 4n + 1\\ & = & 2n^2 + 2n - 4n + 1\\ & = & 2n^2 - 2n + 1\\ \end{eqnarray} $$ So, a hollow pyramid of height \( n \) requires \( 2n^2 - 2n + 1 \) bricks.

For a pyramid of height \(n\), how many bricks do we save by constructing a hollow pyramid rather than a solid one?

The number of bricks saved is equal to the number of bricks in the solid pyramid minus the number of bricks in the hollow pyramid: $$ \begin{eqnarray} & & \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} - (2n^2 - 2n + 1)\\ & = & \frac{2n^3 + 3n^2 + n}{6} - 2n^2 + 2n - 1\\ & = & \frac{2n^3 + 3n^2 + n}{6} - \frac{12n^2}{6} + \frac{12n}{6} - \frac{6}{6}\\ & = & \frac{2n^3 - 9n^2 + 13n - 6}{6}\\ \end{eqnarray} $$

Alternately, notice that making a solid pyramid of height \(n\) hollow involves removing from it a solid pyramid of height \( n-2 \). Then the number of bricks saved is: $$ \begin{eqnarray} \sum_{i=1}^{n-2} i^2 & = & \frac{(n-2)([n-2]+1)(2[n-2]+1)}{6}\\ & = & \frac{(n-2)(n-1)(2n-3)}{6}\\ & = & \frac{2n^3 - 9n^2 + 13n - 6}{6}\\ \end{eqnarray} $$ which is the same thing we obtained using the other method.
